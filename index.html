What Makes a TikTok Shop Go Viral?
Choosing the right product category is the first strategic step for TikTok sellers. This quadrant chart helps you explore the market by comparing two important signals: average GMV (horizontal axis) and average video count (vertical axis) for each category. Use the two sliders to define what counts as “high” in your context. As thresholds move, categories shift between four zones: Cash Cow (high GMV, high video activity), Focused (high GMV, low video activity), Potential (low GMV, high video activity), and Weak (low GMV, low video activity). Bubble colors change automatically based on the zone, making it easy to spot which categories deserve further exploration — whether you're looking for quick wins or long-term growth areas.

Each chart focuses on a different perspective — features distribution, trait composition, and pricing-GMV relationships. This boxplot shows the distribution of product counts among top and non-top shops. It helps compare medians, measure variability, and identify unusual performers across shop types.
The central box area represents the interquartile range (IQR), covering the middle 50% of shops. The bold horizontal line inside the box shows the median product count.
The dots outside the whiskers are outliers — shops with exceptionally high or low product counts. These may indicate standout sellers or niche players.
This stacked bar chart compares top and non-top shops across three operational aspects: creator collaboration, video production, and product variety. These three factors reflect how actively a shop engages with the TikTok ecosystem and can jointly shape its viral potential and GMV performance.
One key success factor for top-performing shops is their extensive collaboration with creators. By partnering with a larger number of influencers, these shops tap into diverse follower bases, gaining broader reach and increased social proof—key drivers of consumer trust and impulse purchases on TikTok.
Another clear difference is in the volume of promotional videos they produce. Top shops consistently generate more video content, increasing their exposure on the platform’s For You feed. More videos mean more entry points for product discovery, higher chances of virality, and sustained engagement over time.
Finally, top shops tend to offer a wider range of products. A broad catalog not only attracts different consumer segments but also encourages repeat visits and cross-selling opportunities, ultimately contributing to higher total GMV and customer retention.

This scatter plot shows the relationship between product price and shop GMV. Each dot stands for a shop, Red dots highlight top-performing shops, while gray dots indicate others. The chart reveals pricing patterns linked to stronger sales outcomes.

Top-performing shops tend to cluster within a moderate price range, avoiding extremes. This points to an optimal pricing zone that balances value and affordability—key to boosting GMV.

Shops with lower average prices are more likely to achieve higher GMV. These are often volume-driven businesses that rely on mass-market appeal and impulse purchases.

In contrast, higher-priced shops tend to underperform in terms of GMV, possibly due to lower conversion rates or limited appeal within the TikTok ecosystem.

Target High-Potential Categories

Insights from the quadrant chart reveal that categories with higher average GMV and more video activity tend to produce stronger shop performance. Choosing the right category not only aligns with TikTok’s content algorithm but also matches user interests, increasing visibility and sales potential.

Increase Product Variety and Content Output

Top-performing shops typically offer a broader range of products and maintain higher video output. A wide catalog attracts diverse buyers, while frequent content helps keep the shop visible in TikTok’s recommendation system. Balancing inventory expansion with active content production is key to sustaining engagement and growth.

Balance Pricing Within Category Context

The scatter plot shows that shops with moderate unit prices tend to achieve better GMV. However, this pattern varies across categories — what is “moderate” in one category may be high in another. Identifying a price point that fits both category norms and consumer expectations is essential for maximizing conversions.
